About Us

The Team

What do we do?

Bluevale's Electric Car Club team is comprised of hard working individuals who all share a common interest and passion for electric vehicles.

Our future designers are learning to think green while creating awareness of the environmental impacts of today's modern technology. We strive to help demonstrate new and exciting green alternatives to fossil fuels while learning and having fun at the same time.

The Bluevale Electric Car Club is a group of over 20 dedicated students who design, build and race electric cars. Our goal is to produce an electric car that is fast as well as energy efficient. The club allows students real world experiences that cannot be obtained through classroom work and textbooks alone. It also provides students the ability to develop teamwork, cooperation and leadership skills. Students also develop their engineering and design skills as they construct and redesign the cars.

Our Accomplishments

Active Participator in Electric Car Club Races

This is Bluevale Electric Car Club's 14th consecutive year in competition. Over the years, many students of various skills and comfort levels have joined the club and grew their skills whether that be technical or interpersonal. We strive to provide an open and fun environment for all students, and this has worked for the last 14 years and many to come.

2016 Winner of the University of Waterloo Championship

At the Bluevale Electric Car club, we raced at the University of Waterloo race in May this last year and won the 12V and 24V races while winning the engineering design award. These awards are the mark of another successful year for Bluevale's Electric Car Club and marks another year of great efforts, dedication and teamwork from the members of the club.

2016 NECA Open Class Winner - Berlin, Michigan

The Bluevale Electric Car Club also competes in the NECA Open Class race in Michigan, bringing a different series of electric vehicles as per the different requirements. At this race, we don't only compete with local schools but schools across all of North America. This race provides a new dynamic to racing and provides a new challenge for our team.

2012-2016 Top Engineering Award at the University of Waterloo

Over the years, Bluevale has established themselves as a fast moving technological high school by constantly developing new technical approaches to solve complex problems. For the last 4 years, the Bluevale Team has won the top engineering award as judged by University of Waterloo staff and volunteers. We are very proud of this accomplishment and wish to continue innovating and bringing new ideas to the races.

2012-2014 NECA Stock Class State Champions

During these years, our Bluevale Electric Car Club team has went down and raced in Michigan against teams across all of North America. During the 2012 to 2014 years, Bluevale won the Stock Class race and has proved that Bluevale is truly amongst the top schools in this sector of racing. Bluevale hopes to continue this legacy over the many years to come as they continue to strive and innovate.

2012-2016 Top 3 Finishes in all University of Waterloo Events

The Bluevale Electric Car Club team is known for their success at the University of Waterloo events. As mentioned to the left, Bluevale has won the engineering award for the last five years. Alongside the engineering design award, Bluevale has also placed top 3 in all the 12V and 24V races through those years, many of which were first place finishes. Bluevale continues to work on their designs and innovate to stay ahead of the other schools.

How to Help

We run purely on the generous donations of companies and individuals.

Without the help and support of the community, we wouldn't be able to have done all the innovative advancements we have already done.

There are a few ways in which you can assist the team:

  • Monetary Support - Allows us to make any purchases necessary to keep the club going.
  • Services - Helping us physically build components of our cars using more advanced machinery then present in a technology classroom at a high school.
  • Equipment or Tools - Providing the team with tools and equipment to carry out tasks at the school.
