About Us

The Team

What do we do?

Bluevale's Electric Car Club team is comprised of hard working individuals who all share a common interest and passion for electric vehicles.

Throughout the year, we split off into smaller groups focused on individual tasks such as the making of a new car, the modification of the older cars, the management of data, programming and designing (technical or graphical). We all play a role in the success of the club, and the making of the cars. As we approach the end of the year, we put our efforts together to create the finished product, a life-size, drivable, electric vehicle.

Our Accomplishments

University of Waterloo Race (2015/16 year)

At the Bluevale Electric Car club, we take part in the University of Waterloo Electric Vehicle challenge. During the 2015/16 year, we won both the 12V and 24V races, along with the Engineering design award.

Michigan Race (2015/16 year)

Another race we attended in the 2015/16 year was the Michigan race. We placed really well this last year, finishing in the top 3 in all the events.

University of Waterloo Race (2014/15 year)

The Bluevale team attended the University of Waterloo race during the 2014/15 year where we did exceptionally well. We finished first in both the 12V and 24V race, while also winning the Engineering design award.

Michigan Race (2014/15 year)

The Bluevale Electric Car team also attended the Michigan race in the 2014/15 school year, where we also did exceptionally, finishing in the top 3 in all the events.

University of Waterloo Race (2013/14 year)

The team also attended the race in Waterloo hosted by the university. This year there was great competition, along with a few issues on our part which resulted in a 2nd and 3rd place finish in the 12V and 24V races. We also won the Engineering design award that year.

How to Help

We run purely on the generous donations of companies and individuals.

Without the help and support of the community, we wouldn't be able to have done all the innovative advancements we have already done.

There are a few ways in which you can assist the team:

  • Monetary Support - Allows us to make any purchases necessary to keep the club going.
  • Services - Helping us physically build components of our cars using more advanced machinery then present in a technology classroom at a high school.
  • Equipment or Tools - Providing the team with tools and equipment to carry out tasks at the school.
